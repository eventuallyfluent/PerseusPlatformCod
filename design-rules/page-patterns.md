# Page Patterns

## Catalog

The catalog is for scanning and comparison. It should show more courses above the fold than a sales page while preserving the Perseus card language.

- Compact collection navigation.
- Compact course cards.
- Clear filters.
- Four-column desktop grids where space allows.
- No hero-scale typography inside product cards.

## Sales Pages

Sales pages can be more atmospheric and spacious.

- Strong hero section.
- Full curriculum accordions for long courses.
- Reviews and instructor proof.
- Clear checkout path.
- Use the editorial section pattern for narrative proof, instructor, FAQ, and curriculum framing sections when it helps the buyer understand the path.

## Public Editorial Sections

Use this pattern for homepage and sales-page narrative sections that explain meaning, trust, or progression. Keep the structure restrained: mono eyebrow, sharp display title, optional italic lavender-to-gold emphasis, muted body copy, thin borders, and compact cards.

Do not use it for catalog grids, checkout form internals, learner dashboards, admin pages, or repeated product cards.

## Student Portal

The student portal follows the GroupApp-inspired model.

- Sidebar or sidebar-like navigation.
- Main content area for progress and continuation.
- Cards show progress, lesson counts, and next action.

## Lesson Player

The lesson player should prioritize learning.

- Dominant video/content area.
- Clear module/lesson navigation.
- Focus mode remains available.
- Video sizing should feel larger than legacy course platforms where possible.
